# SplitStable Positioning

## Purpose

This document defines SplitStable as a product marketing portfolio project. The goal is not to pretend this is already a venture-backed company. The goal is to show how a web3 PMM thinks: audience, wedge, category, message, proof, launch motion, and technical narrative.

## Portfolio Framing

SplitStable is a build-in-public case study for a Solana payments product.

It demonstrates how to take a raw web3 product idea and turn it into:

- a clear target audience
- a differentiated market position
- a credible technical story
- a demo narrative
- a launch plan
- public PMM artifacts

## Category

Primary category:

> Chat-native stablecoin payments for shared expenses.

Plain-language category:

> Expense splitting for Telegram groups that settle in USDC.

Do not position SplitStable as a generic Splitwise clone. That category is crowded and already understood. The stronger category is the intersection of:

- Telegram group coordination
- crypto-native users
- non-custodial stablecoin settlement
- Solana Pay payment flows

## One-Liner

SplitStable helps Telegram groups split expenses and settle in USDC on Solana without copying wallet addresses or downloading another finance app.

Recommended tagline:

## Positioning Statement

For crypto-native groups that coordinate in Telegram, SplitStable is a chat-native expense splitting tool that lets friends, teams, and communities track shared costs and settle in USDC through their own Solana wallets.

Unlike traditional expense apps that stop at IOUs, or Solana bill-splitting apps that start in a separate web or mobile app, SplitStable starts inside the group chat where the expense already happens.

## Primary Audience

### First User

Crypto-native groups that already use Telegram and Solana wallets.

Priority segments:

- Solana hackathon teams
- hacker houses
- digital nomads
- crypto friend groups
- web3 community operators
- small contributor teams

### Why This Audience

These users already have the behaviors SplitStable needs:

- They coordinate in Telegram.
- They understand wallets.
- They are familiar with USDC.
- They often cross borders.
- They cannot rely on one local payment app.
- They care about fast, direct settlement.

## Persona

### "The Group Organizer"

The first PMM persona is the person who pays first and chases everyone later.

Profile:

- coordinates dinners, trips, team expenses, or shared housing
- already uses Telegram for group coordination
- knows enough crypto to use Phantom, Solflare, or Backpack
- dislikes chasing people for repayment
- wants visible proof that people paid

Job to be done:

> When my group shares a cost, I want to create the split where the conversation is already happening, so everyone can pay quickly and I do not have to chase them later.

Pain points:

- manual wallet address sharing
- screenshots as payment proof
- unclear balances
- awkward reminders
- country-specific payment rails
- too many separate apps

## Core Insight

Group expenses start as social coordination, not financial workflows.

Most tools force people to leave the chat after the group has already discussed the expense. SplitStable turns the chat into the coordination layer and Solana into the settlement layer.

## Why Now

- Stablecoins are becoming normal payment primitives for crypto-native users.
- Solana fees make small payments practical.
- Wallet UX is good enough for everyday payments.
- Telegram is already the coordination layer for many web3 communities.
- Existing expense tools either track IOUs or process payments, but rarely do both for crypto-native groups inside chat.

## Why Solana

Solana is not a decorative blockchain choice. It matters because the product needs small, frequent, low-friction settlement.

Key technical proof points:

- SPL token transfers support USDC settlement.
- Solana Pay supports wallet-compatible payment links and transaction requests.
- Reference keys can help track and validate payment intents.
- Fees are low enough for small shared expenses.
- Phantom, Solflare, and Backpack are familiar wallets for the first user segment.

## Why Telegram

Telegram is the wedge because it removes adoption friction.

Instead of asking every friend to install a new finance app, the organizer adds a bot to the chat where the group already coordinates.

Messaging:

> The group chat is already the source of truth. SplitStable makes it payable.

## Product Promise

SplitStable promises:

- fast split creation
- visible balances
- one-tap payment flow
- non-custodial settlement
- no wallet address copying
- no separate expense app for the first interaction

## Messaging House

### Main Message

SplitStable turns Telegram group expenses into wallet-signed USDC payments on Solana.

### Pillar 1: Chat-Native

Users create and manage splits inside Telegram, where the group is already discussing the expense.

Proof:

- `/split 50 USDC dinner`
- group-visible balances
- payment reminders inside chat

### Pillar 2: Stablecoin Settlement

USDC settlement reduces the awkward IOU loop.

Proof:

- users pay from their own wallets
- payments are visible on-chain
- balances update after confirmed transfer

### Pillar 3: Non-Custodial Trust

SplitStable coordinates payments but never holds user funds.

Proof:

- wallet-signed transactions
- verified mint addresses
- transaction validation by reference
- no backend private key for user funds

### Pillar 4: Built For Crypto-Native Groups

The product starts with users who already have wallets and already coordinate in Telegram.

Proof:

- hackathon teams
- hacker houses
- Solana communities
- cross-border groups

## Differentiation

SplitStable is not trying to out-feature Splitwise on day one.

The differentiated wedge:

> Telegram-first UX plus Solana-native USDC settlement.

This separates SplitStable from:

- traditional expense trackers that do not settle crypto payments
- Telegram expense bots that only track IOUs
- Solana split apps that require a separate web or mobile workflow

## What SplitStable Is Not

SplitStable is not:

- a bank
- a custodial wallet
- a remittance company
- a full accounting product
- a custom Solana protocol
- a token project
- a replacement for every local payment app

This constraint makes the PMM story cleaner and safer.

## Proof Points To Build

The first demo needs only a few proof points:

1. A Telegram command creates a group split.
2. A payment button opens a wallet flow.
3. A devnet USDC transaction is signed.
4. The bot validates the transaction.
5. The group balance updates.

This is enough to prove the product narrative.
